University Capstone
Project

Background

The capstone project was presented as a course requirement by The University of Victoria Mechanical Engineering Department. This project was done by the engineering team that consisted of myself, Jersey Kertawidjaja, and three other mechanical engineering students: Duncan Wallace, Nipuna Fernando, and Brandon Nolan. Without my team members and the teaching assistants, this project would much too overwhelming.

Global Solution Universe Inc. (GSU) is recommending their client (which will be referred to as the ‚Äútreatment plant‚Äù to maintain confidentiality) to implement a supplemental aeration system into their existing aeration basin/lagoon, in addition to their already existing surface aerators. The injector nozzle proposed will inject aerated oxygen into the bottom of the lagoon. GSU would be sourcing a venturi nozzle and injector nozzles from Mazzei Injector Company LLC. (MICL), a company that design and build the injectors. Whilst the piping and manifold specifications would be sourced from Engineered Pipe Group (EPG).

Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to design a support chassis for the injector manifold, enabling safe and efficient installation and inspection while the treatment plant continues its operations. The project will address various loading conditions, including operation, installation, and inspection, to ensure the structural integrity of the injector system. The successful implementation of the supplemental aeration system and the ability to inspect and maintain the injectors over at least five years will have profound implications for the client. Firstly, it will enhance the overall treatment efficiency and effectiveness of the plant, leading to improved water quality and environmental preservation. Secondly, the ability to conduct inspections without interrupting operations will significantly reduce downtime and operational costs, ultimately benefiting the client's financial sustainability.

Final Design

The final design of the piping manifold was a culmination of a thorough and comprehensive process that involved simulation analysis, extensive client consultation, and meticulous material selection and research. By leveraging these key factors, the design team was able to develop a robust and optimized solution to meet the project's objectives and requirements.

Throughout the design process, simulations played a crucial role in validating the structural integrity and performance of the support chassis. Advanced simulation tools allowed the team to assess the stress distribution, load bearing capacity, and deflection of the design under two critical loading scenarios. As a result, weak points were identified and addressed early in the design phase, leading to an optimized and reliable final solution. Client consultation and feedback were paramount in shaping the final design. Regular meetings and discussions with the client allowed the design team to gain valuable insights into specific needs and preferences of the end-user. By incorporating the client's input, the final design not only met technical specifications but also aligned perfectly with the client's vision and requirements.
